In each of these paths we examine how planned tree cover changes may interact with these climate changes. A note on interpreting results

L-Range simulations involve several input datasets that may not fully capture the distribution and magnitude of variables of interest (e.g., vegetation cover, soil characteristics). It also includes assumptions related to ecosystem processes and those intrinsic to climate change data used in the simulations. The creation of the 2030 baseline tree cover dataset and any subsequent increases in tree cover do not account for land use and land tenure. Results presented here convey the general direction and spatial distribution of future changes in tree cover under one plausible successful afforestation scenario.

Climate change scenario background

Comprehensive climate change analysis is conducted by the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC), which relies on a set of future pathways that encompass greenhouse gas emissions, and the associated responses of the Earth system, out to the year 2100. These pathways are called the Shared Socioeconomic Pathways (SSPs) and represent a range of social, economic and environmental possibilities for people and planet. More information is available here.
